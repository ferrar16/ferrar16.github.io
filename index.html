THE BASE IDEA OF GPS
Satellites

GPS (Global Positioning System) operates via 24 satellites that orbit around the Earth. These satellites gather information on their position relative to earth as well as relative time in order to provide an accurate location for the user.

Reciever

The reciever is a device that recieves information from satellites in order to calculate your position on Earth. At any time of day and location on the planet, the reciever will consistently have a line of visibility with four satellites.

Trilateration

Information from three of the four satellites is read by the reciever and is used calculates your location. The information used is relative location and time the satellites in relation to the reciever. This process is known as trilateration.
